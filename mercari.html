Mercari App Redesign

UX/UI Design · Mobile App
Mercari is a C2C platform that allows users to buy and sell things straight from mobile devices. This redesign of item details page was aimed to positively impact purchase-rate without negatively impacting the long-term user experience.
The biggest change is to take advantage of the vertical momentum, starting from users' intent of scrolling down to view the item images to browsing the bulk of similar items in the end.

Also, the warm color palette is used not only to increase the sense of sharing and community, but also to impulse users for purchases.

I created the screen itself and also the design guide in Sketch (source file).
Open in Google Drive

Value Proposition

Mercari helps to connect people who want to buy and sell things of emotional value through their mobile phones. And it's safe to say that Mercari is a community-powered marketplace that brings millions of people together.

Therefore, to increase purchase is not simply placing the CTA icons or making the visuals more appealing. Instead, it's more about selling the stories behind the items and sharing emotions within the community, which can also positively impact the long-term user experience.

Current Page Critique

Based on the critique, I decided to tackle two main problems.

(1) Information architecture: figure out better ways to compile, label, organize, and lay out the information that the page needs to incorporate in order to give an ideal user experience that leads to increased sales;
(2) Color palette: use warm colors to better match the app concept to connect, trust, and share.

Images Matter
So Do The Stories

Unlike other e-commerce platform, Mercari is where people sell and buy beloved items. Thus, the text description edited by the seller is just as important as the photos.

Meanwhile, buyers' interests in the photos, can be used to trigger the vertical momentum.

Visual Emphasis

(1) Price with colored background to enhance readability as well as to enrich the user experience.


(2) With larger images, more visible price tags, and enlarged font size of the item title, the thumbnails of the similar items can better help arouse purchasing interests and make decisions.
