UX Research

A comprehensive user experience research on a website that offers small businesses with customized retirement plans and health insurances

0. intro

Our client Ubiquity Retirement, based in San Francisco, is an entrepreneurial FinTech company that provides small businesses and entrepreneurs with customized retirement plans (401K and IRA) and health insurances. The website was redesigned and newly launched at the beginning of 2016 - just one month before we met our client, but the organization of information, information structure and usability are in the process of testing. Therefore, our main focus is to help revise the website from the perspective of user experience.

01. problem

Key Question

How to define good user experience on the Ubiquity website?

The company's products are mainly targeting small businesses including startups with capacity of under 50 employees. To purchase retirement plans for employees is never a decision that can be easily made, so judging the usability of the whole website simply by the purchasing rate seems not so reasonable.

Instead, the measurements that we set were customer’s behaviors that represent their interests in Ubiquity Retirement and its products, such as signing up for 401K plan, chatting with representatives, or leaving their contact information on the website. And thus, our research question was: what would arise users' interests in the retirement plans purchasing website? Or in other words: what effects users' decision making regarding the purchasing process?

02. methodology

Over the two months, we went through a complete UX research process, including interaction map, user interviews, persona creation, surveys, comparative analysis, heuristic evaluation, and usability test. We created a compilation of 6 technical reports and a summary video as our final deliverable.

Interaction Map

  • Documented 20 pages
  • Visualized the site structure
  • Identified three main goals of Ubiquity website: sign up for products, contact the company, and follow up on social media

User Interviews

  • Conducted 30-40 minutes interviews with 1 stakeholder and 4 target users
  • Created 3 personas and 3 scenarios
  • Realized factors other than the product itself that users value: supplementary services, benefits for employees, credibility of the seller, and personal contact.

Comparative Analysis

  • Studied 5 competitors of the Ubiquity website, including direct, indirect, partial, parallel and analogue competitor
  • Brainstormed a list of important dimensions and ranked the dimensions from 1 to 5 in matrix
  • Found that the competitors did better in: the presentation of navigation, product information, trust building, and personal contact.

Survey

  • Categorized the survey flow on Qualtrics for 5 different types of users
  • Reached out to 50 participants and collected 40 replies
  • Quantitive research showed that users care about product information and supplementary services and gave insights about user's preference of personal contact and trust source

Heuristic Evaluation

  • Applied the 10 usability heuristics proposed by Jakob Nielsen
  • Conducted the evaluations both individually and in group
  • The website mostly fails the 2 heuristics: visibility of system status and match between system and the real world.

Usability Test

  • Designed 5 scenario-based tasks concerning key use cases
  • Conducted 5 test with target users
  • Top findings: user expected customized  ways to sort their preference and responsive way to contact with representatives; product pages lack deeper and detailed information

03. key findings

Users are looking for detailed information about the product and more than the product itself.

By synthesizing all research results using the above methods, we concluded 5 major usability issues users faced. Among them, lack of product information might have the most negative influences. (If you are interested in learning about the other 4 issues, please refer to the video in the final or contact me at zhyining@umich.edu.)

User Interviews:
All the senior officials and administrators that we interviewed agreed that purchasing retirement plan for the company is a long-term investment and they expected supplementary services and benefits for employees.

Comparative analysis:
For fee structure and benefits, the product features are all described by text and screenshots of the system on Ubiquity website, while its competitors apply more intuitive ways to send the message to the visitors. 

Pricing page of ForUsAll website
Investment page of Captain401 website

For supplementary service, competitors like Mutual of America provides a list of administrative service, such as employee’s education and enrollment, benefit estimation report and okan summary. However, such supporting information does not exist on the Ubiquity site, which could be a huge competitive disadvantage.

Survey:
Survey results show that fee structure and plan benefits are two most important features people care about . Besides, approximately 70% of the respondents agreed that  supplementary services like product education, annual report and personal consulting are very or moderately important while planning to purchase a retirement plan. 

Survey results of 43 participants on the question: on a scale of 1 to 5, how important are the following retirement plan features?
Survey results of 43 participants on the question: on a scale of 1 to 5, how important are the following supplementary services?

Users are also interested in learning about the product in ways that are more intuitive, easier to understand and less wordy. Results show that graphical representations and investment calculators are most important features.

Usability Test:
Most users felt frustrated that the product pages only contained basic information about that particular plan (e.g. the pricing or the features), and they couldn’t find the detailed information they wanted (e.g. investment options, annual POI or the user interfaces of the application included in the plans). Two users expected to learn about the investment options included in the retirement plan, which was one of the most important perspectives they would rely on to make the purchase decision. However, such kind of information was not presented on the product page.

04. recommendations

For one thing, it is recommended that Ubiquity website include more detailed information to make user's decision making easier.
